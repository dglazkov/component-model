6.1 Overview

By default, the component should not be able to affect the including document or parent component, nor be able to discern its state (confinement), and vice-versa (isolation).

6.1.1 Terminology

Confinement
Prevent a component to reach out, analyze or modify the containing document.
Isolation
Prevent the containg document from reaching into, analyzing or modifying the component, apart from APIs the component itself provides.

6.2 JavaScript

Confinement requires:

  • The component has no access to document or window, or the returned objects are limited.
  • The component cannot access, nor override any prototype used in the outside doucment, including HTMLElement and Object.
  • CSS rules do not cross over into the shadow tree, nor our of it.
  • ...

Encapsulation requires:

  • There is no accessor method to reach the shadow tree from the enclosing scope.
  • The outside document cannot access, nor override the component object, nor any prototype in its prototype chain, including HTMLElement and Object.
  • CSS rules do not cross over into the shadow tree, nor our of it.
  • ...
